Who art in heaven? - By Zekiah

We sit on the stained wet red concrete,
a folly flock of grey and white.
The sky quiet and nice,
only ended by the silent invasion.

They form an eye, circling, swarming over us,
Turning London's dank, polluted air of nothing
into a smog of yellow billed beak, whooshing
winds of the flaps of trillions of feeble
wings, a cream of shit over every
pane and scream.

And one by one lightning smites the earth,
sabers of dislodged feathers, slit down the
wrists and necks of each girl and boy,
ready to meet their maker.

Peck, Peck, Peck. A small lone seagull screeched in a heavenly fury.
As it smashed the brains out of a colossal giant.
Blood spilling like wine, for each holy creature.

After the reckoning, light starts to pierce through
their shroud of feather, casting silver beams
on the redder concrete, as strange forms of
flesh lay dismembered
below.

Like doves they disappeared
into this light.

Fair retributions for our sins,
we had forgotten to feed the seagulls.
But we don't know that they will return again,
tomorrow.
