Once in Princess Selva’s classroom - By Angelos

Oh Christmas eve, Oh Christmas day, From Santa’s beard I’m running away. It’s period 1 and form time’s done, I charge up the stairs and torch that fat. I coil in the lockers under tall guys and girls But the reindeer’s shadow Casts through the door We’re summoned inside Last Christmas is sung Oh holy night, the stars chose to hide. Time for math, were squared and cubed, But where are the tables? They are circles instead. A hundred agonies in icing and jam, From which most kids will pick one or two. But I wander as I wonder right under the desks and remember, remember my insulin levels. Oh come all ye Faithful! Joyful and triumphant This christmas I WON’T give my heart! BECAUSE I’M GLUCOSE INTOLERNT Hark the Herald crisps explode! Glory to, the storm of crumbs! For plastic gifts have been traversed afar The floor is now plastered in chewing gum... So I tell you, my dear friend: On Christmas eve, On Christmas day Forget the sugar, forget the presents, From Santa’s beard should you run away.
