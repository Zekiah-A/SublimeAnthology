OzyAndyas - By Angelos

I met a cyclist from a country road who said:
One oval, hairless head, rolls down the pavement.
At the top of the hill, a battered blazer lies whose sneer,
And slips of late detentions, yet remain scattered
On these weathered slabs.
And on the shattered gates it reads:
"My name is OzyAndyas: King of teachers and students!
Look on my rules, ye naughty and obey!"
Nothing besides remains, round the rusted doors and hollow walls,
The lone and misty woods, stretch far away.
